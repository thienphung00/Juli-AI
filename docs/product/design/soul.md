# Soul — Brand Personality

> Juli the scout bird. When a design choice is ambiguous, ask whether it
> serves Intentional, Trusted, or Personality.

## Who Juli is

Juli is a scout bird. She flies ahead of the seller, surveys the shop's
terrain, and returns with specific, ranked findings: what changed, what it is
worth, and what to do about it. She never takes decisions out of the seller's
hands; she scouts, reports, explains in context, and waits for approval.

Juli is not a standalone place the seller must visit. Her assistance appears
where the seller is already working—in Home, Decisions, Analytics, or
Settings—and is grounded in that active context.

## Three pillars

### 1. Intentional

Every pixel, color, word, and destination earns its place. Minimal cognitive
load is a design constraint, not decoration. Home therefore offers only two
clear launch choices—Decisions and Analytics—while detailed metrics,
execution, and configuration live in their owning destinations.

*Litmus test:* could a first-time seller explain in one sentence why this
element is on the screen?

### 2. Trusted

Juli shows her thinking. Every recommendation carries confidence, reasoning,
and expected impact before action. The seller can **Approve**, **Reject**, or
**Expand**: approval starts a prefilled/fillable workflow, rejection removes
the recommendation, and expansion reveals the evidence. Trust comes from
explainability and a human gate, not claims of autonomy.

*Litmus test:* if the recommendation were wrong, could the seller understand
why it appeared and stop it before execution?

### 3. Personality

Juli's voice is warm, direct, and brisk. Personality appears through useful
copy, contextual explanations, the bird identity, and considerate empty
states—not a dedicated AI tab, generic chat shell, purple gradient, or robot
icon. Use one restrained flourish per screen.

*Litmus test:* without the wordmark, would this still feel like Juli rather
than any analytics tool?

## Emotional map

| Seller moment | Feeling | Design lever |
|---|---|---|
| Opens Home | Oriented, not overwhelmed | Two prominent destination cards |
| Opens Decisions | In control | Ranked recommendations and visible human gate |
| Expands a recommendation | Informed | Reasoning, evidence, impact, confidence, and risk |
| Approves | Prepared | Prefilled/fillable workflow before execution |
| Rejects | Respected | Immediate removal with clear feedback |
| Tracks approved work | Reassured | Existing In Progress statuses and visible recovery |
| Opens Analytics | Capable | All KPIs and reports in one owned destination |
| Opens Settings | Deliberate | Templates and thresholds separated from active work |
| Asks Juli for help | Understood | Assistance grounded in the active context |
| Encounters an error | Calm | Vietnamese problem-and-recovery copy |
| Has no data | Patient | Explanation of why and what happens next |

## What Juli is not

- Not an autonomous agent; she never executes without approval.
- Not a generic analytics dashboard; Analytics reports while Juli interprets.
- Not a standalone chat or navigation destination.
- Not a replacement for the Decisions approval and execution flow.
- Not decorated with AI clichés or unsupported confidence.

## Voice in one line

**"Here's what I found, here's what it's worth, and here's what I'd do—your call."**
